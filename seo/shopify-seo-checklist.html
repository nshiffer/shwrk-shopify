The Complete Shopify SEO Checklist

Everything you need to check across products, pages, blog posts, and collections to make sure search engines can find and rank your store.

Product SEO.

Your products are the most important pages in your store. Every product should have these SEO elements dialed in.

Meta title under 60 characters

Titles longer than 60 characters get cut off in Google search results. Keep them concise and descriptive.

✓Organic Cotton T-Shirt | YourStore38 chars
✗Premium Hand-Crafted Organic Cotton T-Shirt for Men and Women - S...72 chars
Meta description between 50-160 characters

The meta description appears below your title in search results. Too short and you waste space. Too long and Google truncates it.

✓100% organic cotton tee. Pre-shrunk, breathable, and available in 12 colors. Free shipping over $50.
✗Nice shirt.11 chars — too short
Image alt text on all product images

Alt text helps Google understand your images and is required for accessibility. Describe what's in the image, naturally including relevant keywords.

Product description over 300 words

Thin content ranks poorly. A detailed product description with materials, dimensions, use cases, and benefits gives Google enough context to rank you. Aim for 300+ words on your most important products.

Clean URL handles

Use short, descriptive handles: /products/organic-cotton-tee. Avoid auto-generated handles with numbers or SKUs.

Pages, Blog & Collections.

The same rules apply to every resource in your store. Pages, blog articles, and collections all need SEO attention.

Pages

  • ✓ Custom meta title
  • ✓ Meta description 50-160 chars
  • ✓ Meaningful page content
  • ✓ Internal links to products

Blog Articles

  • ✓ Custom meta title
  • ✓ Meta description
  • ✓ 300+ words of content
  • ✓ Featured image with alt text

Collections

  • ✓ Custom meta title
  • ✓ Meta description
  • ✓ Collection description
  • ✓ Collection image alt text

Technical SEO.

Fix broken internal links

Every 404 wastes crawl budget and frustrates customers. Use LinkGuard to scan for broken links and fix them with one-click redirects.

Set up URL redirects for deleted pages

When you remove a product or page, always create a redirect to a relevant alternative. Settings > Navigation > URL Redirects in Shopify admin.

Verify your sitemap

Shopify generates a sitemap at /sitemap.xml automatically. Make sure it's accessible and submit it to Google Search Console.

Optimize page speed

Compress images, use next-gen formats (WebP), minimize apps that inject storefront scripts. Shopify's Online Store Speed report shows your current performance.

Frequently asked questions.

Does Shopify handle SEO automatically?

Shopify provides the infrastructure (sitemaps, canonical URLs, SSL) but you're responsible for the content — meta titles, descriptions, alt text, and product descriptions. Without custom content, Shopify auto-generates titles from product names, which are rarely optimal for search.

How long does it take to see SEO improvements?

SEO changes typically take 2-8 weeks to reflect in search rankings. Technical fixes (broken links, redirects) are usually faster. Content improvements (better descriptions, meta tags) take longer as Google re-crawls and re-evaluates your pages.

Should I customize meta titles for every product?

Ideally, yes — especially for your best-selling and highest-margin products. At minimum, ensure your top 20% of products have custom meta titles and descriptions. Use your product name plus a compelling reason to click.

What SEO score should I aim for?

80+ is strong. 50-79 means there's room for improvement — focus on critical issues first (missing titles and descriptions). Below 50 needs urgent attention. SeoAudit calculates your score based on how many resources have issues relative to your total store size.
