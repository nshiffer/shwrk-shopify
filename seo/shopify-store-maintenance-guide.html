The Shopify Store Maintenance Guide

Your store needs regular maintenance to stay healthy. Broken links pile up, tags get messy, SEO degrades, and pricing gets stale. Here's everything you need to keep your store clean, fast, and optimized.

Why maintenance matters.

Most store owners focus on adding products and running campaigns. But the stores that rank highest, convert best, and are easiest to manage are the ones that get regular maintenance. Broken links hurt SEO. Messy tags break filters. Missing meta descriptions lose search traffic. These problems compound over time.

The good news: maintenance doesn't have to be manual or time-consuming. The right tools automate the boring parts so you can focus on growing your business.

Four areas of maintenance.

Broken Links

Dead links frustrate customers and hurt your search rankings. Scan your products, pages, blog posts, and navigation menus for 404 errors, archived products, and draft content references.

Read the guide → Try LinkGuard →

Product Tags

Duplicate tags, typos, and dead tags clutter your admin and break your storefront filters. Audit your tags regularly to merge duplicates and remove unused ones.

Read the guide → Try TagTidy →

SEO Health

Missing meta titles, short descriptions, no alt text, and thin content all drag your search rankings down. Run regular SEO audits and track your score over time.

Read the checklist → Try SeoAudit →

Pricing & Discounts

Discount codes add friction and kill conversions. Automatic discounts — volume pricing, VIP tiers, and free shipping — apply at checkout without any codes.

Maintenance schedule.

Weekly

✓Run LinkGuard scan (or verify automatic scan completed)
✓Review and fix any new broken link issues
✓Check support email for merchant inquiries

Monthly

✓Run TagTidy audit — merge any new duplicates
✓Run SeoAudit — check score trend, fix critical issues
✓Review discount performance — active discounts still relevant?
✓Clean up seasonal tags and expired promotions
✓Check Shopify admin for draft pages or unpublished content

Quarterly

✓Review all URL redirects — remove outdated ones
✓Audit all product descriptions for thin content
✓Review image alt text across all products
✓Review discount strategy — adjust tiers based on sales data
✓Check Google Search Console for crawl errors

Annually

✓Full tag cleanup — remove all unused and single-use tags
✓Complete SEO audit with score benchmark for the year
✓Review and update privacy policy and terms of service
✓Review all installed apps — remove unused ones
✓Update theme and check for deprecated features

Frequently asked questions.

How much time should I spend on maintenance?

With automated tools, weekly maintenance takes about 15 minutes — review scan results and fix critical issues. Monthly deep-dives take about an hour. The key is consistency, not volume.

What should I prioritize first?

Start with broken links — they directly hurt both customer experience and SEO. Then tackle SEO meta titles and descriptions for your top products. Tag cleanup and discount optimization can come after the fundamentals are solid.

Do I need all four apps?

Not necessarily. Start with the area that's most relevant to your store. If you have 500+ products, TagTidy will save the most time. If SEO traffic is important, start with SeoAudit and LinkGuard. DiscountIQ is best when you're ready to optimize pricing.
